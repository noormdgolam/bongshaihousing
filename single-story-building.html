Advanced Construction Technology

The Smart Choice in Modern Building

A single-story composite building is a modern structure that combines multiple materials — most commonly steel and concrete — to maximize efficiency, durability, and cost-effectiveness.

In this construction method, steel members are used for their tensile strength, while concrete is utilized for its compressive strength, allowing the materials to work together as a single, stronger unit.

This innovative approach makes composite buildings ideal for both residential and commercial use — providing a structure that is stronger, lighter, and more economical than traditional single-material construction.

Technical Excellence

Common Composite Elements We Use

Our composite buildings use three primary structural elements, each carefully engineered for maximum strength and longevity.

🧱

Steel-Concrete Composite Floors

Concrete is poured over profiled steel decking to create a floor slab. Shear connectors bind the concrete and steel together, allowing them to act compositely and create a more robust floor system.

⚙️

Composite Beams

A steel beam connected to a concrete slab forms a single, stronger unit. In slim-floor systems, steel beams are embedded within the floor slab to reduce overall floor depth while maximizing strength.

🏛️

Composite Columns (CFT)

Concrete-filled steel tubes (CFT) use steel as formwork for concrete. The concrete is contained by the steel, increasing compressive strength and providing excellent fire resistance.
